## Summary (EN)
Ceva announced Ceva-Waves UWB, which it positions as the industry's first IEEE 802.15.4ab-compliant UWB IP core, on 10 March 2026. As licensable IP (rather than a finished SoC), it lets chipmakers add 4ab-class ultra-wideband to their own designs. Ceva claims up to 30× extended ranging and 4× faster data rates versus prior-generation UWB, delivered without increasing power consumption or silicon footprint, and fully backward compatible with existing IEEE 802.15.4z deployments.

The headline mechanism is multi-millisecond (MMS) ranging that combines UWB-based ranging with narrowband-assisted architecture — the same tightly-coupled "narrowband assists UWB" approach that 802.15.4ab standardizes. Ceva frames this as moving secure ranging from short "back-pocket" proximity (digital car key, phone-as-key) to reliable "behind-the-wall" and infrastructure-scale positioning. The IP also targets richer protocol stacks: high-accuracy real-time location systems (RTLS), low-latency wireless peripherals, advanced audio transport, and combined ranging-and-radar sensing.

The IP is available for licensing now and integrates with Ceva's wider connectivity portfolio (Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, cellular IoT, 5G). It is the IP-core counterpart to the silicon-side 4ab signal already in the KB (STMicroelectronics' ST64UWB, the first 4ab SoC): together they show 802.15.4ab moving from draft into commercial silicon and licensable IP simultaneously.

该 IP 现已开放授权，并与 Ceva 更广泛的连接 IP 组合（蓝牙、Wi-Fi、蜂窝 IoT、5G）集成。它是 KB 中已有的 4ab 硅片侧信号（意法半导体 ST64UWB，首颗 4ab SoC）的 IP 核对位：两者共同表明 802.15.4ab 正同时从草案走向商用硅片与可授权 IP。

## Why it matters / what's new (EN)
This is the IP-licensing half of the 802.15.4ab commercialization story. The KB already records the first 4ab SoC (ST64UWB); Ceva being first to a licensable 4ab IP core means the standard's enhancements — narrowband-assisted MMS ranging, longer range, ranging-and-radar fusion — will propagate into many third-party chips rather than staying in one vendor's silicon. The "back-pocket → behind-the-wall → infrastructure-scale" framing is the practical thesis for why 4ab matters: it pushes UWB from short proximity unlock toward true infrastructure positioning and sensing.
