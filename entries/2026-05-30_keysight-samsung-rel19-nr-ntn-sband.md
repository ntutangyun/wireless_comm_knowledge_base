## Summary (EN)
On 8 January 2026 Keysight Technologies and Samsung Electronics announced a live demonstration of standardized 3GPP Release 19 New Radio Non-Terrestrial Network (NR-NTN) direct-to-device connectivity over the n252 S-Band. The point of the milestone is that it validates *standardized* satellite-to-device protocols — the Rel-19 NR-NTN specification — rather than the proprietary per-operator architectures that dominated early direct-to-cell deployments, moving the ecosystem toward multi-vendor interoperability.

Keysight supplied the end-to-end emulation environment needed to reproduce orbital dynamics — Doppler shift and long, time-varying propagation delays — against which Samsung's NTN-capable modem validated the physical and protocol layers of the Rel-19 spec. The demonstration exercised satellite-to-satellite mobility with advanced handover so that a moving LEO constellation can hand a session between orbital nodes without dropping it, and confirmed direct-to-device operation where a standard unmodified smartphone connects without specialized hardware. The companies reported no quantified throughput/latency figures, framing it as a feasibility-and-protocol-maturity milestone.

Both sides plan deeper interoperability testing through 2026, targeting reference designs for manufacturers integrating satellite connectivity into consumer and industrial IoT devices. For the KB this is the test-and-validation counterpart to the Rel-19 standards-freeze narrative, and a concrete sign that S-band NR-NTN is approaching commercial readiness.
