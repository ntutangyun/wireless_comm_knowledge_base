## Summary (EN)

TGbn closed the May 2026 Antwerp interim with its agenda at revision 12 (DCN 764r12), reflecting heavy parallel activity across 11+ CR clusters. The LB291 comment resolution is now estimated at ~70% complete, with the group targeting **Draft 2.0 in July 2026**. The closing-plenary uploads on May 14–15 are dominated by revised LB291 CRs (most at rev 3–8), plus two structurally new threads: a **L4S liaison from ARC SC** (DCN 1078r0) bringing IETF low-latency congestion-control considerations into the TGbn scope, and the continued maturation of **Memory Management for Cross-BSS CSI** and **P2P TWT modification**. The MAPC Common Info CR (DCN 290r8) reached its 8th revision — among the most-iterated contributions of the interim. The DBE (Dynamic Beamforming Enforcement) CIDs part 4 thread reached revision 24 (DCN 16r24), confirming the DBE framework is approaching closure.

## Why it matters / what's new (EN)

TGbn's pace this interim confirms the D2.0-in-July timeline is holding rather than slipping further. The L4S liaison (DCN 1078) is the most consequential single document of the closing days — it connects the IETF L4S low-latency congestion-control framework to the Wi-Fi 8 latency architecture, a topic already tracked in the KB via `2026-05-03_nsdi-law-802-11-low-latency-link-layer`. The MAPC Common Info thread (290r8) and the P-EDCA thread (210r8) are both within striking distance of resolution, meaning the two largest remaining LB291 clusters may clear before July. DBE at r24 is effectively done — the remaining iterations are editorial only. The cross-BSS CSI memory management thread (982r0) is the one to watch for a possible dedicated entry next run: it surfaces the storage-cost side of MAPC-class coordination that no prior KB entry has addressed.
