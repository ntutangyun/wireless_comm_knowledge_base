## Summary (EN)

Following HPE's acquisition of Juniper Networks (closed late 2025), HPE Discover unveiled the integration plan for the two enterprise WLAN management platforms: **Aruba Central** and **Juniper Mist**. The two platforms are being merged into a single "AI-native network brain" with shared capabilities rolling out throughout Q1 2026. The strategy is described internally as **"build once, deploy twice"** — common functionality is built into a unified codebase, then surfaced through both Aruba and Mist UIs while the products converge.

For enterprise Wi-Fi engineers this is the largest WLAN-management consolidation event of the post-Wi-Fi-6 era. Aruba Central and Mist had been the two leading AI-driven WLAN management platforms in their respective camps; HPE now controls both. CEO Antonio Neri's framing — that Wi-Fi 7 deployments will be the first generation managed under the unified platform — anchors the consolidation against the Wi-Fi 7 deployment surge already underway (see `2026-05-01_wifi7-deployment-milestone-2026.md`).

The competitive position is also clearer: HPE Aruba and Cisco are now the two-horse race in enterprise WLAN per IDC's market-leader designations, with Cisco answering with its **G300 AI networking chip** and **AgenticOps** management suite, plus liquid-cooled Nexus 8000/9000 switches due in H2 2026.

## Why it matters / what's new (EN)

- **Largest enterprise-WLAN consolidation of the decade.** When the buyer of every cycle's leading-vendor announcement is *the same vendor*, the management-plane diversity story for enterprise WLAN gets a step thinner. Worth tracking what happens to interoperability with non-HPE access points.
- **AI-native management isn't optional any more.** Both HPE and Cisco's responses centre on agentic AI in the management plane — implicitly raising the floor for any enterprise WLAN sale.
- **Customer-side decision pressure.** Enterprises currently running mixed Aruba/Mist fleets now have a roadmap (and a vendor preference) to consolidate. Procurement decisions through 2026 will be made with this convergence in mind.
