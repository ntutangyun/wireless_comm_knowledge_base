## Summary (EN)
The US FCC is considering two rule changes that could substantially improve 6 GHz Wi-Fi performance indoors. The first involves incorporating Building Entry Loss (BEL) into Automated Frequency Coordination (AFC) calculations for indoor standard-power 6 GHz APs, allowing higher transmit power by accounting for the natural attenuation of signals through building walls. The second proposes raising the Low Power Indoor (LPI) power spectral density limit from 5 dBm/MHz to 8 dBm/MHz.

On BEL: while some AFC services have already received waivers to add 6 dB for BEL, the Wi-Fi industry argues the correct value should be at least 20.5 dB — the median established by the ITU — with some advocates pushing for closer to 30 dB to account for modern energy-efficient construction materials (e-glass, better insulation). This would translate directly into higher indoor speed and coverage for standard-power 6 GHz APs.

On LPI: the original 5 dBm/MHz limit was set based on extreme caution. A 2020 CableLabs study verified that 8 dBm/MHz would have no discernible adverse effect on incumbent users. A newer study by a coalition of tech vendors has since confirmed that conclusion, providing strong technical arguments for the increase.

The FCC issued its Notice of Proposed Rulemaking on 6 GHz improvements in February 2026. Industry groups including WiFiForward and a coalition of tech vendors are united behind these changes. Implementation is expected before end of year.

## Key technical points (EN)
- **BEL in AFC**: Incorporate building entry loss (~20-30 dB) into AFC propagation models for composite APs (combined LPI + standard-power), enabling higher indoor transmit power while protecting incumbents
- **LPI power spectral density**: Raise from 5 → 8 dBm/MHz; backed by CableLabs (2020) and tech-vendor coalition studies showing no adverse effect on incumbents
- **FCC NPRM**: Issued February 2026; the LPI increase was not in the original NPRM scope but the FCC invited supplementary proposals
- **Composite APs**: New device class combining LPI and standard-power operation; BEL-based AFC would apply to these
- **Stakeholders**: WiFiForward, Wi-Fi Alliance, New America Foundation (Michael Calabrese), coalition of tech vendors

## Why it matters / what's new (EN)
This is the first concrete FCC movement on 6 GHz rule improvements since the initial 6 GHz unlicensed authorization. The BEL issue in particular has been a long-standing gap — the ITU median of 20.5 dB has been known for years but never incorporated into US AFC rules. If adopted at the higher end (~25-30 dB), indoor 6 GHz APs could operate at effectively outdoor power levels indoors, dramatically improving speed and range. The LPI increase from 5 to 8 dBm/MHz is a more incremental change but still meaningful for the vast majority of consumer 6 GHz devices that operate in LPI mode. Together, these changes would make 6 GHz Wi-Fi significantly more competitive against wired alternatives for high-throughput indoor use cases.
