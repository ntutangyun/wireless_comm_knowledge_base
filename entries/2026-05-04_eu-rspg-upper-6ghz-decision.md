## Summary (EN)

The European Commission's **Radio Spectrum Policy Group (RSPG)** issued its final opinion on the **upper 6 GHz band (6 425-7 125 MHz, 700 MHz total)** at its 12 November 2025 plenary, ending a multi-year split between the Wi-Fi industry and the mobile-cellular operators. The decision: **540 MHz reserved for licensed mobile-cellular use, 160 MHz frozen until WRC-27, zero MHz for unlicensed Wi-Fi**. Combined with the already-allocated lower 6 GHz (5 925-6 425 MHz, 500 MHz unlicensed), the full European 6 GHz picture is now: lower 500 MHz for Wi-Fi, upper 540 MHz for cellular, upper 160 MHz under technical evaluation through WRC-27 (Nov 2027).

The Wi-Fi industry response — Wi-Fi Alliance, Wi-Fi NOW, the European wireless ISP coalition — was sharply negative, with the Alliance calling the recommendation "short-sighted." The mobile camp (GSMA, the German federal regulator) argued the 540 MHz mobile assignment is the floor below which Europe cannot build "advanced, resilient and sovereign" mobile networks (read: 6G). The 160 MHz freeze means there's no second chance until WRC-27 at the earliest, and even then it'd require a CEPT harmonisation decision and Member-State implementation — realistically 2028+.

The practical consequence for Wi-Fi: in Europe, **Wi-Fi 7 and Wi-Fi 8 will operate on 500 MHz of 6 GHz**, half of what the FCC made available in the US (1 200 MHz) and far less than what the Wi-Fi industry positioned its product roadmaps around. 320 MHz Wi-Fi 7 channels and the multi-RU MLO designs in 802.11be will run into a tighter spectrum corridor than originally planned.

对 Wi-Fi 的实际影响：在欧洲，**Wi-Fi 7 与 Wi-Fi 8 将仅有 500 MHz 6 GHz 频谱可用** —— 仅为美国 FCC 释放的 1 200 MHz 的一半，远少于业界产品路线图原本设计针对的容量。802.11be 的 320 MHz 信道与多 RU MLO 设计将运行在比原计划更窄的频谱走廊里。

## Key technical points (EN)

- Upper 6 GHz total: 700 MHz (6 425-7 125 MHz).
- Decision (12 Nov 2025): 540 MHz mobile / 160 MHz frozen / 0 MHz Wi-Fi.
- Combined with lower 6 GHz: 500 MHz total Wi-Fi spectrum in EU vs. 1 200 MHz US.
- 160 MHz frozen until WRC-27 (Nov 2027) earliest.
- Implementation path: RSPG opinion → CEPT technical conditions → Commission harmonisation → Member State.
- 320 MHz channels and MLO designs in 802.11be / 802.11bn run in tighter EU corridor.

## Why it matters / what's new (EN)

This is the regulatory bookend on a question the Wi-Fi industry was openly losing through 2024-2025 (see the Computer Weekly / Wi-Fi NOW coverage in the URL). It does not affect WG 802.11 standards work directly — TGbe and TGbn specifications are global — but it changes the **deployable** capacity in Europe by roughly half vs. North America. Three knock-on effects worth tracking: (1) Vendor SKU strategy diverges — EU-region Wi-Fi 7 / 8 APs will need different default channel-bonding and MLO operating modes; (2) The 160 MHz frozen tranche becomes a long political fight through WRC-27 (2027); (3) 6G mobile operators in Europe will deploy 540 MHz of 6 GHz for cellular, raising practical Wi-Fi/5G NR-U coexistence concerns at the band edges (cf. `arxiv-llm-6ghz-wifi-nru-coexistence`).

## Why it matters / what's new (ZH)

这是 Wi-Fi 业界 2024-2025 年间公开苦苦争取却节节失利的议题的最后一棒（参见 Computer Weekly / Wi-Fi NOW 在该 URL 的覆盖）。它不直接影响 802.11 工作组的标准工作（TGbe 与 TGbn 规范是全球性的），但把欧洲的**可部署**容量较北美减少了大约一半。三条值得跟踪的连锁影响：(1) 厂商 SKU 策略分化 —— 欧盟版 Wi-Fi 7 / 8 AP 将需要不同的默认信道捆绑与 MLO 运行模式；(2) 冻结的 160 MHz 将成为通往 WRC-27（2027）的一场漫长政治战；(3) 欧洲 6G 移动运营商将在 6 GHz 部署 540 MHz 蜂窝，使带边的 Wi-Fi / 5G NR-U 共存在工程上更紧迫（参 `arxiv-llm-6ghz-wifi-nru-coexistence`）。
