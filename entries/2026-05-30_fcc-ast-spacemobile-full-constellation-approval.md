## Summary (EN)
On 21 April 2026 the U.S. FCC granted AST SpaceMobile authorization to operate its full direct-to-smartphone constellation — up to 248 satellites — providing broadband-class supplemental coverage from space (SCS) to ordinary unmodified phones. The approval is a major step toward AST competing head-to-head with SpaceX's Starlink Direct-to-Cell, and it lands AST a U.S. operating footprint to pair with its AT&T and Verizon partnerships, which supply the terrestrial cellular frequencies the satellites reuse to keep subscribers connected outside terrestrial coverage.

Critically, the FCC authorization covers the lower-band 698–960 MHz cellular spectrum rather than the mid-band spectrum AST had acquired the prior year — a band choice that favors link budget and building penetration for direct-to-device service but constrains capacity relative to mid-band. The grant comes with conditions: interference safeguards (toward terrestrial and adjacent satellite operators), radio-astronomy protections, and orbital-debris / space-safety mitigation obligations. The approval was clouded by a launch setback affecting AST's BlueBird ramp-up, so authorization-to-coverage will lag the paperwork.

For the KB this complements the existing AST Q1-2026 readout entry (`2026-05-12`) with the regulatory milestone that actually unlocks U.S. commercial service, and sharpens the Starlink-vs-AST direct-to-device contest the satellite bin tracks.

## Summary (ZH)
2026 年 4 月 21 日，美国 FCC 批准 AST SpaceMobile 运营其完整的直连手机星座——最多 248 颗卫星——从太空向普通未改装手机提供宽带级的补充覆盖（SCS）。该批准是 AST 与 SpaceX 星链 Direct-to-Cell 正面竞争的重要一步，使 AST 获得美国运营版图，并与其 AT&T、Verizon 合作相配套——后者提供卫星复用的地面蜂窝频率，以在地面覆盖之外保持用户连接。

关键在于，FCC 批准覆盖的是低频段 698–960 MHz 蜂窝频谱，而非 AST 上一年获取的中频段——这一频段选择有利于直连终端的链路预算与建筑穿透，但相对中频段限制了容量。批准附带条件：干扰保护（对地面与邻近卫星运营商）、射电天文保护，以及轨道碎片/空间安全缓解义务。该批准因影响 AST BlueBird 星座扩张的发射挫折而蒙上阴影，因此从获批到实际覆盖将滞后于文书进度。

对本知识库而言，本条目与现有 AST 2026 年一季度读报条目（`2026-05-12`）互补，补上真正解锁美国商用服务的监管里程碑，并强化卫星分箱所追踪的星链 vs AST 直连终端之争。
