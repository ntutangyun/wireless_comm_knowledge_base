## Summary (EN)

Following the Beijing Auto Show 2026 centralized debut covered in `2026-05-21_sparklink-digital-car-key-beijing-autoshow.md` and the ecosystem overview in `2026-05-21_sparklink-ecosystem-2026-overview.md`, two May 2026 developments push the SparkLink car-key story from "showcased" into "scaled at the device side." This entry captures the device-side rollout and the SparkLink 2.0 (car-key) performance step.

**(1) HarmonyOS 6.0 → ~50M phones gain ICCE-SparkLink car key.** Huawei has confirmed that the rollout of HarmonyOS 6.0 brings SparkLink-based car-key support — under the **ICCE** (Industry Cooperation Committee for ECU) digital-key framework — to multiple existing handset and watch families via OTA, not just newly launched silicon. The named SKUs:
- **Phones**: Pura 80 series (first to natively ship the feature), with Pura X and Mate 70 gaining it via HarmonyOS 6.0 upgrade. The cited industry-side estimate from late 2025 forecast ~**50 million Huawei smartphones eligible** for OTA-enabled SparkLink car key — the May 2026 device-page documentation is the operationalisation of that forecast.
- **Watches**: WATCH 5 (incl. 10th-anniversary edition), WATCH GT 6 series, WATCH Ultimate 2 series, WATCH ULTIMATE DESIGN variants, WATCH GT Runner 2, WATCH FIT 5.

**(2) Supported vehicles, May 2026 snapshot.** Per the Huawei device-page, paired vehicle support currently covers the Huawei-affiliated SUV/sedan brands: AITO (问界) M7/M8/M9; Luxeed (智界) S7/R7; Maextro (尊界) S800; Stelato (享界) S9 EREV / S9T / S9 2026; Sharedi (尚界) H5 — all 2025+ models. The broader Beijing Auto Show showcase (covered in the May 21 entry) named 20+ vehicle models across Dongfeng, BAIC, SAIC, JAC, Chery, Geely, GAC, GAC Toyota, etc., so ICCE compatibility extends beyond the Huawei-house brands — Huawei's own device-page lists only the in-house combos.

**(3) Features supported**: three primary use cases — vehicle greeting/welcome on approach, unlock-on-approach, walk-away auto-lock — the standard "passive entry" flow that BLE / UWB car keys already deliver but with SparkLink-grade ranging precision and the sub-μs sync from SLB.

**(4) SparkLink 2.0 (car-key version) performance step.** Industry reporting confirms the 2.0 car-key version launching in 2026 delivers vs 1.0: **5× ranging refresh rate**, **+50% detection distance**, **+20% measurement accuracy**. The implication is that 1.0 deployments will get successor silicon / firmware that closes the remaining gap to UWB ranging precision while preserving SparkLink's coexistence / power advantages.

## Why it matters / what's new (EN)

The May 21 Beijing Auto Show entry captured supply-side momentum (20+ vehicle models showcased). This entry captures the demand-side enabler — **HarmonyOS 6.0 turning 50M existing Huawei phones into SparkLink car-key clients via OTA**, without requiring users to buy new hardware. That's the inflection that takes the deployment from "interesting demo at trade show" to "addressable consumer base in the tens of millions." For the SparkLink ecosystem this is the missing piece between (a) ISLA's Beijing Innovation Lab / OpenLab certification infrastructure, (b) the OEM showcase, and (c) actual car-owner adoption. The **SparkLink 2.0 (5× refresh / +50% distance / +20% accuracy)** numbers are also the first concrete generational-improvement metrics for a SparkLink mode — previously the "2.0" framing existed mostly at the alliance-spec level. The 2030 80.8% digital-key penetration forecast (cited from Nov 2025 industry analysis) sets the addressable market context — if it holds, SparkLink digital car key is competing for that ~80% slot against UWB-CCC and BLE-CCC alternatives, with the Huawei-driven Chinese OEM supply chain as its native advantage.
