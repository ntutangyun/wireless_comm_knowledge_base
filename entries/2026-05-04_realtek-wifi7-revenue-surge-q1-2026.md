## Summary (EN)

Digitimes' 4-May-2026 report on Realtek's Q1 2026 results (Jay Liu, Taipei) names **Wi-Fi 7 adoption acceleration as the primary driver** of the company's revenue growth this quarter. Headline number: NT$36.42 billion (~US$1.15B) in Q1 revenue, with "early customer pull-in orders" boosting Q1 demand and pulling forward into Q2. Most of the article body is paywalled, but the lede is itself a useful data point: Realtek is one of the secondary Wi-Fi silicon vendors (not in the Qualcomm / Broadcom / MediaTek front-three but a real share-holder in consumer router and mid-range AP chipsets), and a quarter-on-quarter revenue surge attributed *specifically* to Wi-Fi 7 SKUs is independent confirmation that the consumer-and-mid-range tier is hitting the same Wi-Fi 7 transition that `extreme-networks-wifi7-momentum-q3` and `cisco-wifi7-breakout-year-2026` describe at the enterprise tier.

The "early customer pull-in" detail matters: it suggests OEMs are placing forward orders to lock supply, which is consistent with the DDR-memory-shortage narrative on the platform side (Extreme's call mentioned DDR locked through FY27). Realtek warned of "higher order uncertainty in the second" half — i.e., they read the Q2 pull-forward as borrow-from-future demand rather than a sustained step-up — but a single double-digit-billion NT$ quarter explicitly attributed to Wi-Fi 7 is a meaningful supply-side signal.

## Why it matters / what's new (EN)

The KB lacked supply-side data points on Wi-Fi 7 adoption outside the front-three silicon vendors. Realtek's Q1 print fills that gap: a secondary chipset vendor seeing record demand explicitly attributed to Wi-Fi 7 confirms that the transition is broad (not just enterprise top-of-stack) and is being supply-constrained by upstream memory rather than demand. Together with `extreme-networks-wifi7-momentum-q3` (37% unit-share) and `cisco-wifi7-breakout-year-2026` (40% on Wi-Fi 5, refresh window now), this is the Q1-Q2 2026 picture: Wi-Fi 7 is the active deployment generation across all enterprise + consumer tiers, even as `qualcomm-wifi8-mwc2026` / `mediatek-filogic8000-ces2026` / `broadcom-wifi8-ces2026` already pivot the silicon roadmap to Wi-Fi 8.
