## Summary (EN)

Amazon's LEO broadband programme — renamed from **Project Kuiper to Amazon Leo** during the rebrand — has crossed the 300-satellite mark as of April 30 2026, after 11 missions in the first year of full-scale operational deployment. That makes Amazon Leo the **third-largest LEO constellation in orbit**, behind Starlink and OneWeb. The next launch is **Mission LA-07, May 29 2026 at 7:33 PM EDT** on a ULA Atlas V 551 from Cape Canaveral SLC-41 — payload is 29 satellites. Launch cadence has been roughly one mission every ~5 weeks throughout April 2026, executing against a deeply over-contracted launch backlog (Amazon has secured >100 launches across SpaceX, ULA, Arianespace, and originally Blue Origin).

The hard regulatory clock is the **FCC license: half the constellation (~1,618 satellites) must be in orbit by July 30 2026, full constellation by July 30 2029**. At 300 satellites mid-May with ~6 weeks to the July deadline, Amazon is not on track to hit the half-constellation milestone by the original deadline — public coverage suggests Amazon will need an FCC waiver or schedule extension. The Q1 2026 earnings call (Andy Jassy) signalled commercial service launch in **mid-2026** for Canada, France, Germany, UK, and US, which appears decoupled from the half-constellation milestone and instead tracks "minimum viable coverage" — i.e., Amazon plans to start serving paying customers with substantially less than half the constellation in orbit.

The hardware-side news: **the Amazon Leo router became visible via FCC filings in May 2026**. Specifications are ~6 in × 6 in (significantly smaller than Starlink's standard router) — a design choice consistent with Amazon's targeting of residential customers who already own a router and want a discrete unit. Aviation/enterprise variants ("gigabit-speed") are referenced but not yet detailed in the May 2026 update.

## Why it matters / what's new (EN)

The KB's LEO-constellations thread has been Starlink-heavy. This entry adds the concrete Amazon-side numbers for the first time in this scout's run: 300 satellites, 11 missions, ULA-dominated cadence, July 30 2026 half-constellation deadline that won't be met. The **service decoupling from the half-constellation milestone** is the strategic news — Amazon is opting for "minimum viable coverage" mid-2026 launch rather than waiting for the FCC threshold, which signals it will accept revenue-loss-from-coverage-gaps over revenue-loss-from-late-launch. The **6×6 inch router form factor** is meaningful for the consumer-installation experience: Amazon is positioning Leo as a residential add-on (compact, fits anywhere) rather than a primary connectivity hub (Starlink's standard router is significantly larger). Combined with the AST SpaceMobile entry (`2026-05-24_ast-spacemobile-q1-2026-45-bluebirds.md`) this captures the bracketing of the LEO-broadband market in May 2026: Starlink at scale (~7,000 sats, multi-million MAU), Amazon Leo deploying (300 sats, mid-2026 service), AST SpaceMobile direct-to-device (working with ~45-60 sat threshold).
