## Summary

MediaTek used CES 2026 (January 2026) to debut the Filogic 8000 chipset family, its Wi-Fi 8 / 802.11bn product line. The announcement was timeline-only; deep technical specs and per-SKU performance claims were not detailed. MediaTek positions the family as "later this year" availability — implying second-half 2026 shipping.

Coupled with Qualcomm's FastConnect 8800 / Dragonwing NPro A8 Elite at MWC 2026 (see `2026-05-01_qualcomm-wifi8-mwc2026.md`), MediaTek's Filogic 8000 establishes that *both* major mobile-WiFi silicon vendors will have Wi-Fi 8 silicon shipping into the supply chain before the standard is even ratified (May 2028 SA Ballot per `2026-05-01_ieee-tgbn-d2-schedule.md`). This is the same pattern as Wi-Fi 7: silicon ships against draft, devices launch against late draft, certification follows ratification by ~12 months.

MediaTek's announcement is corroborating evidence of the timeline; it doesn't itself add new technical surface. The interesting follow-up is the Filogic 8000 spec sheets when they appear — they will reveal which D1.x features MediaTek is implementing (i.e., considers stable enough to bake into silicon) and which are deferred (i.e., still in flux).

## Key technical points

- **Family name:** MediaTek Filogic 8000
- **Announced:** CES 2026 (January 2026)
- **Availability:** "Later in 2026" — second half implied
- **Targets:** routers, mesh APs, gateways (typical Filogic positioning)
- Detailed specs not yet public

## Why it matters / what's new

Confirms the second major Wi-Fi 8 silicon platform is in the pipeline alongside Qualcomm's. Two-vendor signal is a good proxy for "this generation is real and on schedule." Worth a follow-up scout run when MediaTek publishes the Filogic 8000 datasheets — those will reveal the specific 802.11bn D1.x features MediaTek considers locked.
