## Summary

Geraci et al. (Dec 2025 v2) is a comprehensive 25-year retrospective covering IEEE 802.11b through emerging 802.11bn. The author list is a who's-who of the academic Wi-Fi community (UPF, INESC, Nokia Bell, Ikerlan, AMD, Rice, NSF). It is a tutorial / survey, not a primary research paper, but is the single best onboarding document for anyone joining the field from outside.

The most current value is in **two appendices**: the AI/ML appendix (Appendix D) explicitly tracks the IEEE 802.11 AI ML SC's exploratory work — the same activity that, per separate reporting, has now spawned the AI Offload Study Group at the March 2026 plenary. The 802.11bn section confirms: "low-latency and high reliability [are] first-class citizens", with MAPC, ELR PPDU, and unequal-modulation as the headline new features.

The tutorial framing makes it a useful background-citation source and a fast onboarding read for newcomers to 802.11.

## Key technical points

- Coverage: 802.11b → 802.11bn, all generations
- Sections: spectrum allocation, PHY, MAC, multi-user, energy savings, MLO, MAPC
- Appendix D: AI/ML in 802.11 — automation, resource allocation, traffic prediction
- Confirms 802.11bn priorities: low-latency + reliability via MAPC, ELR PPDUs, unequal modulation
- 11 co-authors spanning academia + industry (UPF, INESC, Nokia Bell, Ikerlan, AMD, Rice, …)
