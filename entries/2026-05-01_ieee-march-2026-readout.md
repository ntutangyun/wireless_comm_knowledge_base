## Summary

The March 2026 IEEE 802.11 plenary in Vancouver produced four substantive outcomes:

**1. AI Offload Study Group approved.** First formal Wi-Fi AI standardization effort. Scope: standards enabling APs to function as edge AI compute nodes for sub-20-ms latency-sensitive applications. The SG begins formal operations at the May 2026 interim in Antwerp. This is the earliest opportunity for outside contributions to influence the SG's initial scope.

**2. 802.11bq (Integrated mmWave) PPDU finalized.** The TGbq PPDU structure was deliberately mirrored on sub-7 GHz Wi-Fi designs to lower chipmaker porting costs. MAC scheduling discussions advanced two paths — TWT-based (predictable) vs ICF/ICR-based (on-demand) — without formal motions. The MAC scheduling side is therefore still flexible.

**3. 802.11bn (Wi-Fi 8) D2.0 slipped from May to July 2026.** ~60% of D1.0 comments resolved. 100+ comment-resolution submissions covered MAPC and coordinated beamforming. The May 2028 ratification target survives.

**4. Wi-Fi 9 (post-bn) Study Group expected July 2026.** Six contributions seeded discussion of >10 Gbps throughput, sub-10ms latency, industrial-robotics + XR use cases. Task group formation expected mid-2027; completion ~2032.

## Key technical points

- **AI Offload SG:** approved March 2026; first interim May 2026 (Antwerp); scope = AP as edge AI compute node, sub-20ms targets
- **TGbq mmWave:** PPDU locked, MAC scheduling (TWT vs ICF/ICR) still open
- **TGbn:** D2.0 → July 2026; May 2028 ratification target stable; MAPC + coordinated BF active
- **Wi-Fi 9 prep:** 6 contributions, formal SG expected July 2026 plenary, work mid-2027 → ~2032

## Why it matters / what's new

The most consequential standards-activity entry of the run. Three takeaways:

1. **AI Offload SG window is now open.** This is the first IEEE 802.11 study group whose scope is explicitly "AP as edge AI compute node". The May 2026 Antwerp interim is the first window for inbound contributions to shape SG scope; afterwards the work-in-progress will start hardening.
2. **TGbq mmWave MAC scheduling is still flexible.** TWT-based vs ICF/ICR-based on-demand access is one of the most open design questions in 802.11 right now.
3. **MAPC continues to be the most active TGbn comment cluster.** Independently confirms the November 2025 MAPC-LLM paper (`2026-05-01_arxiv-mapc-llm-agents.md`) is on the most-active research surface.
