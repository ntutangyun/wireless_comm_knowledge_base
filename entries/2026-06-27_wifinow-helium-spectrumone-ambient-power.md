## Summary (EN)
Wi-Fi NOW's 26 Jun 2026 roundup links three deployment-side signals that belong together in the KB: Helium's Wi-Fi offload footprint reaching Redondo Beach, Charter launching Spectrum One, and ambient-power wireless moving from standards-track discussion toward product maturity. The item is not a single standards contribution; it is a market-readout that shows Wi-Fi continuing to absorb traffic and device classes at the network edge.

The Helium and Charter threads are useful because they frame Wi-Fi as managed access infrastructure rather than only home or enterprise LAN plumbing. Carrier offload, municipal coverage, and broadband-bundled Wi-Fi all depend on operational coordination across AP fleets, identity, policy, and backhaul. The ambient-power thread points in the opposite direction of the device spectrum: very low-energy Wi-Fi-class devices that need discovery, triggering, receive procedures, and practical coexistence with ordinary APs.

The novelty here is the juxtaposition: the shipping ecosystem is stretching Wi-Fi upward into carrier-grade managed coverage while 802.11bp-style ambient-power work stretches it downward into batteryless or energy-harvested endpoints. Tracking both in one entry helps connect the commercial deployment story with the AMP-IoT standards work already covered in the Mentor TGbp digests.
