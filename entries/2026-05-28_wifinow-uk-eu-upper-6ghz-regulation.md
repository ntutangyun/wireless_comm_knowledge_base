## Summary (EN)
This Wi-Fi NOW analysis (by CEO Claus Hetting) lays out the diverging regulatory paths for the upper half of the 6 GHz band (roughly 6425–7125 MHz) in Europe versus the UK. In November 2025 the EU's Radio Spectrum Policy Group (RSPG) issued a recommendation to reserve the upper 6 GHz for potential future IMT (licensed mobile / 6G) use — effectively leaving it fallow for Wi-Fi. In January 2026 the UK's Ofcom proposed an alternative: open the band to Wi-Fi now while protecting any future mobile services through Automated Frequency Coordination (AFC), prioritising mobile in specific licensed areas if and when it materialises.

The crux is AFC. Ofcom's proposed system is essentially the same automated-coordination mechanism already certified by the FCC and operating in the US and Canada, where a database gates Wi-Fi transmit power/locations to protect incumbents. Applied to the upper 6 GHz, AFC would let indoor and outdoor Wi-Fi devices use the band immediately while remaining subject to future restrictions should licensed mobile gain priority in a given area — what Ofcom frames as using available spectrum productively now without foreclosing future mobile use. Ofcom notes the band underpins next-generation Wi-Fi capacity (the wide 80/160/320 MHz channels of Wi-Fi 6E and Wi-Fi 7) as well as a possible pathway to 6G.

The piece marshals industry arguments against the RSPG's reserve-for-IMT stance. The Wi-Fi Alliance (Alex Roytblat) argues full-band 6 GHz access is needed to realise the value of Europe's fibre investment, while 6G use of the band is speculative and capital-intensive and mobile-operator economics are under pressure. Cisco (Pelin Salem) frames spectrum restriction as a compounding opportunity cost — higher enterprise deployment costs and slower introduction of AI into enterprise infrastructure, putting EU sectors such as defence, healthcare and the public sector at a structural disadvantage. None of the recommendations are final; both remain under active debate.

## Summary (ZH)
这篇 Wi-Fi NOW 分析（作者为 CEO Claus Hetting）梳理了欧盟与英国在 6 GHz 频段上半部分（约 6425–7125 MHz）上分道扬镳的监管路径。2025 年 11 月，欧盟无线电频谱政策组（RSPG）建议将上 6 GHz 预留给未来可能的 IMT（授权移动 / 6G）使用——实际上对 Wi-Fi 闲置该频段。2026 年 1 月，英国 Ofcom 提出替代方案：现在即向 Wi-Fi 开放该频段，同时通过自动频率协调（AFC）保护未来可能的移动业务，并在特定授权区域、当移动业务真正出现时给予其优先权。

关键在 AFC。Ofcom 拟用的系统本质上与 FCC 已认证、并在美加运行的自动协调机制相同：由数据库限定 Wi-Fi 的发射功率/位置以保护既有用户。用于上 6 GHz 时，AFC 可让室内外 Wi-Fi 设备立即使用该频段，同时在某区域未来授予授权移动业务优先权时受相应限制约束——Ofcom 将其表述为"既现在就高效利用频谱、又不排除未来移动使用"。Ofcom 指出该频段既支撑下一代 Wi-Fi 容量（Wi-Fi 6E 与 Wi-Fi 7 的 80/160/320 MHz 宽信道），也是通往 6G 的一条可能路径。

文章汇集了反对 RSPG"预留给 IMT"立场的产业观点。Wi-Fi 联盟（Alex Roytblat）认为，要兑现欧洲光纤投资的价值需全频段 6 GHz 接入，而该频段的 6G 用途仍属推测、投入巨大，且移动运营商经济性承压；思科（Pelin Salem）则把频谱限制视为不断累积的机会成本——抬高企业部署成本、拖慢 AI 进入企业基础设施，使欧盟的国防、医疗、公共部门等处于结构性劣势。各项建议均非最终结论，仍在广泛讨论中。

## Key technical points (EN)
- Band in dispute: upper 6 GHz, ~6425–7125 MHz (Ofcom's fuller picture includes Wi-Fi priority access for 6425–6585 MHz).
- EU RSPG (Nov 2025): recommend reserving upper 6 GHz for potential future IMT (6G) — closed to Wi-Fi.
- UK Ofcom (Jan 2026): open to Wi-Fi now, protect future licensed mobile via AFC, prioritise mobile in relevant areas later.
- AFC = the same Automated Frequency Coordination already FCC-certified and live in US/Canada; database-gated Wi-Fi power/location to protect incumbents.
- Rationale for Wi-Fi access: enables wide 80/160/320 MHz channels for Wi-Fi 6E/7; leverages European fibre; 6G use of band still speculative.
- Industry voices: Wi-Fi Alliance and Cisco against the reservation; webinar featured Ofcom, Wi-Fi Alliance, Cisco.
- Status: neither recommendation final; ongoing debate.

## Why it matters / what's new (EN)
The KB's 6 GHz spectrum-policy coverage has so far been US-centric (e.g. 2026-05-21_fcc-6ghz-rule-changes on FCC indoor-power changes). This adds the European dimension — the RSPG-vs-Ofcom split over whether the upper 6 GHz goes to Wi-Fi or is held for 6G — which is the single biggest open question for Wi-Fi's mid-term capacity in Europe. The technical hinge is that AFC, already field-proven in North America, is now the proposed sharing tool for a contested band in the UK, signalling AFC's spread from incumbent-protection (US 6 GHz standard power) to future-mobile-protection. Novelty is low (regulatory positions, not a new mechanism), but it materially broadens the spectrum-policy picture beyond the US.

本 KB 此前的 6 GHz 频谱政策内容偏美国（如 2026-05-21_fcc-6ghz-rule-changes 关注 FCC 室内功率调整）。本条目补上欧洲维度——RSPG 与 Ofcom 在"上 6 GHz 归 Wi-Fi 还是留给 6G"上的分歧——这是欧洲 Wi-Fi 中期容量的最大悬而未决问题。技术关键在于：已在北美实地验证的 AFC，如今被提议用作英国某争议频段的共享工具，标志着 AFC 从"保护既有用户"（美国 6 GHz 标准功率）扩展到"保护未来移动业务"。其新颖性较低（监管立场而非新机制），但实质性地把频谱政策图景从美国扩展到了欧洲。
