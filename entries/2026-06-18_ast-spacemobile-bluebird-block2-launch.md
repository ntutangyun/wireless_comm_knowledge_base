## Summary (EN)

On **17 June 2026**, a SpaceX Falcon 9 lifted off from Space Launch Complex 40 at Cape Canaveral and delivered AST SpaceMobile's first three **Block 2 BlueBird** satellites — **BlueBird 8, 9, and 10** — into low Earth orbit. This is the post-launch milestone the satellite scout had flagged across many prior runs: the move from launch-logistics to actual on-orbit direct-to-device (D2D) capability. AST switched this batch from Blue Origin's New Glenn to Falcon 9 after an April New Glenn mishap deployed an earlier satellite into the wrong orbit.

The Block 2 satellites are a step change in physical scale. Each carries a **communications phased-array antenna up to ~2,400 square feet (≈223 m²)**, which AST states makes them the **largest commercial satellites ever deployed in low Earth orbit**. The larger aperture is what enables direct connection to **unmodified standard smartphones** at the link budgets D2D requires. Each Block 2 satellite supports **up to ~120 Mbps peak data rate** and is designed to deliver roughly **10× the bandwidth capacity of the Block 1 BlueBirds** already on orbit.

This launch advances AST toward its stated **2026 deployment target of ~45 satellites in orbit**, the threshold the company has said enables continuous SpaceMobile service across the US, Europe, Japan, and other strategic markets (see the 2026-05-24 Q1 readout entry, which captured the pre-launch context and the earlier 98.9 Mbps peak D2D measurement over international waters). The three satellites will now go through orbit-raising and commissioning before contributing to beta and commercial D2D service later in 2026 / into 1H-2027.

## Key technical points (EN)

- **Launch**: SpaceX Falcon 9 from SLC-40, Cape Canaveral, 17 June 2026; payload = BlueBird 8, 9, 10 (first Block 2 trio).
- **Aperture**: up to ~2,400 sq ft (~223 m²) phased-array per satellite — largest commercial LEO satellites by AST's claim.
- **Capacity**: up to ~120 Mbps peak per satellite; ~10× the bandwidth of Block 1.
- **Direct-to-device**: connects unmodified standard smartphones (no special hardware) — the large aperture closes the D2D link budget.
- **Deployment context**: progresses toward AST's ~45-satellite 2026 target; switched to Falcon 9 after the April New Glenn wrong-orbit mishap.

For the sat-direct-device bin it marks the transition from "approved and funded" to "deployed hardware," and sets up the next watch item: the first real on-orbit throughput / coverage measurements from the Block 2 trio (orbit-raising + commissioning over the coming weeks), which would be a higher-novelty performance entry than this deployment milestone.
